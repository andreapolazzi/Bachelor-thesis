Setup

Let’s do another EDA, but this time let’s see if there are any differences correlating telomere length to variables such as TF rates, TVR norm. by all reads or TVR norm. by intratel. reads.

First of all, we must load the dataset and the required packages:

library(readxl)
library(ggplot2)
data <- read_xlsx("../data/raw/drivers.xlsx")

And rename/remove duplicate columns, along with awkwardly named ones:

source('../scripts/clean_columns.R')
clean_data <- clean_drivers_data(data)

Let’s create the subset that we need (the column tel_reads/total reads can be considered as a representative of telomere length):

tel_data <- unique(

    'tumor_tf_rate',

Correlation between Telomere Length and all other features

Telomere Length vs TVR norm. by total reads

We choose Spearman as our correlation method since it deals with monotonic, non-parametric relationships. Pearson’s correlation has some assumptions that do not hold true in most biological data: normality and absence of outliers

ggplot(tel_data, aes(`tel_reads/total reads`, tvr_all)) +
  geom_point(alpha=0.2) +
  geom_smooth(method = "lm", se = T)

cor.test(tel_data$`tel_reads/total reads`, tel_data$tvr_all, method = 'spearman')
## 
##  Spearman's rank correlation rho
## 
## data:  tel_data$`tel_reads/total reads` and tel_data$tvr_all
## S = 3010208153, p-value < 2.2e-16
## alternative hypothesis: true rho is not equal to 0
## sample estimates:
##       rho 
## 0.7033497

We can see that telomere length shows much more correlation with TVR norm. by total reads than TF rates does (ρ = 0.703 vs ρ = 0.093). This strong positive correlation makes sense: samples with longer chromosomes will have an higher proportion of TVRs over the total reads.

Here we see a weak, negative relatioship (ρ = -0.074), almost flat, suggesting that TVR density inside telomeres is largely independent of telomere length.
